Status Capital Building Society endorses the principles contained in the King IV report on corporate governance and conﬁrms its commitment to the principles of fairness, accountability, responsibility and transparency as advocated therein. The Board strives to ensure that the group is ethically managed according to prudently determined risk parameters and in compliance with generally accepted corporate practices and conduct. The Board has established controls and procedures to ensure that the accuracy and integrity of the accounting records are enhanced and maintained, and to provide reasonable assurance that assets are safeguarded from loss or unauthorized use. The Board has to ensure that the society is at all times solvent and run in a prudent manner.


SECURING THE FUTURE

The controls and procedures that are put in place ensure that the ﬁnancial statements may be relied upon for maintaining accountability for assets and liabilities and preparing the ﬁnancial statements.

The Board is committed to ensuring that the interests of members are protected and safeguarded by adopting the necessary risk management policies to ensure that the building society is managed soundly and that risk is adequately monitored, managed and mitigated and that compliance with any market conduct or prudential requirements is prioritised.

KEY COMPLIANCE AREAS INCLUDE:


  • Avoid being used to facilitate money laundering or the ﬁnancing of terrorism. Client due diligence, KYC, AML risk assessments.
  • Complying with the Consumer Credit Act in all lending and to avoid reckless lending.
  • Managing and mitigating market risk, liquidity risk, concentration risk, regulatory risk and systemic risk.
  • Maintaining prudent capital adequacy ratios.
  • Adequate and timely regulatory reporting, quarterly, annually, etc.
  • It is the responsibility of management to maintain systems integrity and to implement controls and give effect to board strategy.

    • Strategic decision-making will be enhanced and guided by three vital committees namely:

  • Remuneration Committee
  • Credit Committee
  • Risk and Audit Committee
